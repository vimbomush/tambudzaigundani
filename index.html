About Me

Who Am I?

Hi I'm Tambudzai Gundani. I am Policy Data Scientist based in Washington, D.C.,.

At the crossroads of a global pandemic and a policy reform debate, I found myself analyzing disaster-related tweets using natural language processing, not just to parse data, but to understand human need in real time. That moment solidified my belief: data isn't just numbers, it's a lifeline for smarter decisions, fairer systems, and sustainable futures.

A few months ago, while reviewing a spreadsheet of projected employment data, I found myself wondering: what if this wasn't just a file we cleaned, but a living map of skills that could power smarter decisions, by educators, policymakers, and students alike? That question now drives much of my work.

With a background that bridges data science, regulatory policy, and global business strategy, I've made it my mission to turn complex data into clarity and actionable insight. Currently pursuing an M.S. in Data Science and a Graduate Certificate in Regulatory Policy & Practice at The George Washington University, I bring a multidisciplinary perspective shaped by an MBA in Global Business and hands-on experience across sectors.

My toolkit spans Python, R, SQL, Tableau, Power BI, and machine learning, technologies I've applied to projects with real-world impact: from predictive modeling of asthma prevalence using Random Forest and boosting techniques, to building healthcare data warehouses and traffic forecasting systems. These aren't just academic exercises, they're strategic tools to inform policy, optimize operations, and enhance public well-being.

In parallel with academic work in Big Data Analytics and NLP, I explore how curriculum and labor market needs align, working toward systems that are not only intelligent but equitable.

Professionally, I've led initiatives at GE Healthcare to boost inventory efficiency by 35% and cut lead times by 40%, and at Huawei, where I scaled social media analytics and automated data pipelines for measurable engagement gains.

Currently, I lead the AIHub Team for Research & Innovation at the National Accreditation Commission. I'm co-developing a cloud-native, AI-enhanced platform that aligns projected employment trends with academic standards, leveraging OpenAI, AWS, and SST to transform the future of education and workforce strategy.

I thrive where data meets purpose, whether that's regulatory innovation, evidence-based public policy, or building intelligent decision systems for the greater good.
